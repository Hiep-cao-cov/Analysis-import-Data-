Tab 1 : Market Size & Landscape
Understand the total addressable market in Vietnam for chemical raw materials — specifically MDI (PMDI,..) , and TDI — to benchmark Covestro's current position and identify headroom for growth.
Question 1: What is the total annual import volume (kg) and value (USD) for PMDI, and TDI in Vietnam from 2022 to 2025 — and how is the market growing?
Why these matters
This is Supplier's TAM (total addressable market) baseline. If the market grew 15% YoY in PMDI or TDI imports but selected supplier's volume only grew 5%, you're losing share. If the market shrank but supplier selected held flat, you're gaining.
Question 2: What is selected supplier’s current market share by volume (kg) in each product category — and how has it trended year by year?
Why these matters
A declining share in a growing market means competitors are taking new customers before you can. A stable share in a shrinking market means you're both losing — you need to find why the category is contracting.
Question 3: Is the total market import volume seasonal — are there peak quarters where demand spikes and Supplier selected can prepare better supply/pricing offers?
Why these matters:
PMDI or TDI demand often peaks before Lunar New Year (furniture) and mid-year (mattress/sofa season). If you know customers are stocking up in Q3, you can approach them in Q2 with forward pricing deals before competitors do.

Question 4: How many unique importing companies exist in the market — and what is the concentration ratio (do the top 10 buyers account for >80% of volume)?
Why these matters:
If 10 companies buy 80% of all PMDI or TDI in Vietnam, supplier selected only needs to win (or not lose) those 10 accounts to control its revenue. This determines whether you need a mass-market or key-account sales strategy.
